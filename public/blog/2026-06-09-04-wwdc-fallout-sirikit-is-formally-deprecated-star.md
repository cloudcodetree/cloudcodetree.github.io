A second-day developer detail from WWDC that wasn't in the keynote-level coverage: Apple gave SiriKit a formal deprecation notice and made the expanded **App Intents** framework the *only* way an app can integrate with the new Gemini-powered Siri. Apps still using the older `INExtension`/intent classes keep compiling under iOS 27 but throw deprecation warnings and — critically — won't surface in the rebuilt Siri at all, since it routes exclusively through App Intents. The removal window is ~2–3 years (roughly macOS 29 / fall 2028), but the functional cutoff is now: any app relying on SiriKit voice features is already invisible to the new assistant. If you own an iOS/macOS app with Siri or Shortcuts integration, schedule the App Intents port this cycle rather than treating it as deprecated-but-fine. *New since the June 8/9 briefings, which covered Siri/Gemini, iOS 27 third-party assistants, and Xcode 27 but not the SiriKit deprecation.*
