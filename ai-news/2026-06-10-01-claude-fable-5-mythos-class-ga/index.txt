12:T77a,Anthropic released **Claude Fable 5** on June 9 — the first model from its **Mythos-class tier** (above Opus) cleared for general use. Anthropic calls it state-of-the-art on nearly every benchmark it tested, and the launch-day partner quotes back that up: Hex reported the first-ever 90% on its core analytics benchmark, and Rakuten credited Fable's self-validation for making "highly autonomous operations possible."

The mechanism worth understanding before you build on it: Fable 5 ships with classifiers watching for **cybersecurity, biology/chemistry, and model-distillation** requests. When one trips, your query is **silently answered by Claude Opus 4.8 instead**, with a disclosure. Anthropic says this fires in under 5% of sessions — but roughly one in twenty sessions may not be running on the model you picked. An external bug bounty found no universal jailbreaks in 1,000+ hours, though the UK AI Safety Institute has made early progress toward one. New for everyone: Mythos-class traffic now carries a **mandatory 30-day data retention**, even for enterprises with zero-retention agreements.

The practical details: API access via `claude-fable-5` is live today at **$10/$50 per million tokens** — double Opus 4.8. Fable 5 is included free on Pro, Max, Team, and seat-based Enterprise plans **only through June 22**; on June 23 it moves to usage credits. The sibling **Mythos 5** (cyber safeguards lifted) stays gated to government and critical-infrastructure partners via Project Glasswing.
