12:T996,**Fable 5 in GitHub Copilot exits its 50% weekly usage cap on July 7 — full access restores, but it bills at Anthropic token rates and requires 30-day data retention. Set a hard spending cap before you run agents.**

**Fable 5 cap ends July 7.** When Fable 5 was re-enabled in GitHub Copilot on July 1 (following the US export-control lift), access was capped at 50% of weekly usage limits. That cap ends July 7. Copilot Pro+, Max, Business, and Enterprise users will have uncapped access to Fable 5 from that point. Two things to know before enabling it:

- **Token-rate billing, not flat credits.** Fable 5 bills at Anthropic's published rates ($10/MTok input, $50/MTok output) under Copilot's usage-based plan — not the flat premium-request credits used for Sonnet or Opus. A single long agentic session can run $50–500+ depending on context size and iteration count.
- **Data retention required.** Anthropic's safety classifiers for Fable 5 require retaining prompts and outputs for up to 30 days. All other Claude models in Copilot (Opus 4.8, Sonnet 4.5, Haiku 4.5) still operate under Zero Data Retention.

**Agentic billing warning.** Since Copilot moved to token-based AI Credits on June 1, teams running agentic sessions on frontier models are reporting bills 10x–50x above their previous flat-rate spend. GitHub's own research found agentic tasks can consume ~1,000× more tokens than single-turn queries; at token-rate pricing, an unmonitored overnight agent run can exhaust a month's budget.

**Action:** Go to **Settings → Billing → GitHub Copilot → spending limit** and set a hard cap now — before July 7, before running any Fable 5 agent session.

**Why it matters:** Fable 5 is the most capable model in the Copilot picker; it's now fully unleashed. The billing model means it's also the most expensive, with no ceiling unless you set one.
