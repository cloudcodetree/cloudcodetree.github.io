12:T836,**TL;DR: Anthropic launched Claude Tag today — @Claude in any Slack thread summons a shared agentic teammate that plans tasks, reads channel context, and proactively flags updates across your whole team.**

**Claude Tag**, now in research preview for Enterprise and Team customers, is Anthropic's first persistent Slack agent. Type `@Claude` in any thread:

- **Execute multi-step tasks independently.** Claude reads the conversation, breaks the request into stages, completes them, and delivers the result to the team — not just to the person who asked.
- **Proactive monitoring.** Tell Claude to watch a channel and surface posts that match a condition ("flag anything that touches the payment API") — it does so without being re-prompted.
- **Shared identity.** Every member interacts with the same Claude "identity." Half-finished tasks can be handed off; context is shared across the team, not siloed to individuals.

Anthropic says the Claude Code team has used Claude Tag internally all year. It now writes 65% of the product team's code, including most of what built Claude Tag itself.

**Note: June 23 outage.** Earlier today the API, Claude.ai, Claude Code, and Claude Cowork experienced two 529-error (overloaded) incidents, peaking at 8,000+ Downdetector reports. Both were resolved within an hour; Anthropic has not published a root-cause analysis.

**Why it matters:** Claude Tag is Anthropic's push to make Claude a visible team participant in the tool every engineering team already lives in — not just a terminal tool for individuals. If your team uses Claude Code, this makes Claude's work visible and handoff-able in Slack without a context switch.
